US Troops in the United Arab Emirates

Following a joint U.S.-Israeli military assault on Iran that began on February 28, 2026, Iran retaliated by launching drones and ballistic missiles at U.S. military bases and civilian infrastructure across the region, including targets in the United Arab Emirates. In response, the UAE implemented a partial closure of its airspace, with major airlines such as Etihad Airways suspending flights to and from Abu Dhabi due to the regional disruption caused by the conflict

By Easton Heroux

The United Arab Emirates has been a cornerstone of U.S. military strategy in the Middle East for decades, with the American troop presence surging after 9/11, before dropping off dramatically following 2020 as the U.S. drew down forces across the region. However, numbers are sharply on the rise again as Iran has actively targeted U.S. assets across the Gulf, including bases within the UAE, pulling America back into a significant military posture in the region.
